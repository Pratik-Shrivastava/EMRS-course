## **Introduction**
- The **National Education Policy of India 2020 (NEP 2020)** was approved by the **Union Cabinet** on **29 July 2020**.
- It outlines the **vision of a new education system in India**, replacing the **National Policy on Education, 1986**.
- The **language policy** in NEP 2020 is advisory in nature ‚Äî states, institutions, and schools have autonomy in implementation.
- Education in India is a **Concurrent List subject**.
- The **Ministry of Education** formed a **committee under Dr. K. Kasturirangan** to draft this new policy.

---

## **What is the National Education Policy 2020?**
- Approved on **July 29, 2020**, NEP 2020 is the **first education policy of the 21st century** in India.
- It replaces the **National Policy on Education (NPE) 1986**.
- NEP 2020 proposes reforms in **school, higher, and technical education** aligned to **21st-century needs**.
- It focuses on **five foundational pillars**:  
  **Access, Equity, Quality, Affordability, and Accountability**.
- The policy aligns with the **2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development**.
- Aim: To transform India into a **knowledge society and global superpower** through **holistic, flexible, and multidisciplinary education**.
- Emphasis on **bringing out unique capabilities** of each student.

---

## **Features of NEP 2020**
- Addresses challenges of **Quality, Affordability, Equity, Access, and Accountability**.
- Reforms education **from early childhood to higher education**.
- Focuses on:
  - Strengthening **teacher training**.
  - Reforming the **exam system**.
  - Improving **early childhood care**.
  - Restructuring the **regulatory framework**.
- Other goals include:
  - Increasing **public investment** in education.
  - Setting up the **National Education Commission (NEC)**.
  - Promoting **vocational and adult education**.
  - Strengthening **use of technology** in education.

## **Important Highlights**

### **Policy Development**
- Replaces **National Policy on Education, 1986**.
- 2015: Committee under **T.S.R. Subramanian** initiated the process.
- 2019: Draft submitted by **Dr. K. Kasturirangan Committee** (ISRO Chief).
- Over **2 lakh suggestions** received from **676 districts**.

### **Key Highlights**
1. **Universal Education**: Ensure access to education for all from preschool to high school by **2030**.
2. **Bringing Back Dropouts**: Target to bring back **2 crore out-of-school children**.
3. **New School Structure**: Shift to **5+3+3+4 model** (12 years of schooling + 3 years preschool/Anganwadi).
4. **Basic Learning Skills**: Focus on **reading, writing, and arithmetic** in early years.
5. **Vocational Education**: Introduce **job skills from Class 6** with internships.
6. **Local Language Teaching**: Medium of instruction **till Grade 5** in mother tongue/local language.
7. **Assessment Reforms**: Continuous assessment instead of rote memorization.
8. **Higher Education Growth**: Aim for **50% Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER)** in higher education by **2035**.
9. **Flexible Curriculum**: Students can **choose and change subjects** in college.
10. **Multiple Entry/Exit**: Students can **enter or leave** at various stages with certification.
11. **Academic Credit System**: Credits transferable between institutions.
12. **Research Promotion**: Establish a **National Research Foundation**.
13. **Regulation and Autonomy**: Create a **single regulator** for higher education (excluding medical and legal).
14. **Reduced Affiliation System**: Gradually end college affiliation within **15 years**.
15. **Technology Integration**: Set up a **National Educational Technology Forum (NETF)**.
16. **Equity and Inclusion**: Ensure **equal opportunities** for all, with focus on **disadvantaged groups**.
17. **Multilingual Learning**: Encourage **learning of multiple languages**, including **Pali, Persian, and Prakrit**.

## **Benefits of NEP 2020**

1. **Universal Access** at all levels of school education.
2. **Early Childhood Care & Education** with new **5+3+3+4 structure**.
   - Includes age group **3‚Äì6 years** under school curriculum.
3. **Foundational Literacy & Numeracy Mission** for early learning.
4. **Foreign University Collaboration**: Allows **global institutions** to set up campuses in India.
5. **Reformed Curriculum and Pedagogy**:
   - Flexibility in subjects.
   - Removal of rigid separations (arts/science, curricular/extracurricular).
   - Vocational education from **Grade 6**.
6. **Teacher Education & Training**:
   - 4-year integrated **B.Ed.** program.
   - New **National Curriculum Framework for Teacher Education (NCFTE)**.
7. **Multilingualism**:
   - **Mother tongue** as medium till Grade 5 (preferably till Grade 8).
   - **Sanskrit** as optional language at all levels.
8. **Assessment & Accreditation**:
   - Establish **PARAKH** ‚Äì Performance Assessment, Review, and Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development.
9. **Higher Education Reforms**:
   - Holistic, multidisciplinary undergraduate programs.
   - **Academic Bank of Credit** for flexible learning.
   - **National Research Foundation (NRF)** to strengthen research.
10. **Regulation**:
    - **Higher Education Commission of India (HECI)** as umbrella body (excluding medical/legal).
11. **Technology in Education**:
    - **NETF** for innovation and planning in digital education.
12. **Promotion of Indian Languages**:
    - Establish **Indian Institute of Translation and Interpretation (IITI)**.
    - Institutes for **Pali, Persian, and Prakrit**.
13. **Internationalization of Higher Education**:
    - Encourage **Indian universities abroad** and **foreign universities in India**.

## **Challenges in Implementation**

1. **Infrastructure Gaps**: Lack of adequate facilities across states.
2. **Teacher Training Needs**: Shortage of trained teachers.
3. **Digital Divide**: Only 30% population has smartphone/internet access.
4. **Funding Constraints**: Target of **6% GDP** for education difficult to achieve.
5. **Enormity & Diversity**: India‚Äôs vast education network complicates implementation.
6. **Administrative Capacity**: Limited institutional strength at state and central levels.
7. **Mother Tongue Implementation**: Difficulties due to **22 official languages** and multiple dialects.

## **2Ô∏è‚É£ School Curriculum and Vocational Education**

- **Vocational Education:** Starts from **Grade 6 (Middle Stage)**.
  - Includes a **10-day bagless period** (internships with local artisans like carpenters, potters, etc.) during Grades 6‚Äì8.
- **Coding:** Introduced in **Class 6** as part of Middle Stage activities.
- **Language Policy:**
  - Medium of instruction until at least **Grade 5** (preferably till Grade 8) should be **home/mother tongue/local language**.
- **3-Language Formula:**
  - Flexible; at least **two of the three languages must be native to India**.
- **Sanskrit:** Offered as an **optional subject at all levels** of school and higher education.

---

## **3Ô∏è‚É£ Assessment and Evaluation (PARAKH)**

- **Shift in Focus:** From rote learning to **competency-based assessment** testing analysis, application, and critical thinking.
- **Board Exams:**
  - Redesigned to be **simpler** (testing core capacities).
  - Conducted **twice a year** ‚Äì one main and one improvement exam.
- **PARAKH:**
  - Full Form: **Performance Assessment, Review, and Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development.**
  - A **National Assessment Centre** will be established.
- **Progress Card:**
  - A **360¬∞ multidimensional report**, including **self, peer, and teacher assessments**.

---

## **4Ô∏è‚É£ Teachers and Training**

- **Minimum Qualification:** By **2030**, the minimum degree qualification for teachers will be a **4-year integrated B.Ed.**
- **Continuous Professional Development (CPD):**
  - Every teacher to complete at least **50 hours of CPD training annually.**
- **NPST (National Professional Standards for Teachers):**
  - A **common guiding framework** to be developed by **2022**.

---

## **5Ô∏è‚É£ Equity and EMRS Specifics**

- **SEDGs (Socio-Economically Disadvantaged Groups):** NEP focuses on equitable education for marginalized communities.
- **Gender Inclusion Fund:**
  - For **girls and transgender students** to ensure equitable quality education.
- **Special Education Zones (SEZs):**
  - Declared in regions with high SEDG populations.
- **Tribal Education:**
  - **Ashramshalas:** Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE) to be introduced in tribal schools.
  - **NCC Wings:** Encouraged in secondary schools in tribal-dominated areas.
